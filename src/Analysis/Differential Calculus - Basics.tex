In this chapter we recall, prove and discuss some Differential Calculus related results, theorems and exercises.

Throughout the chapter, $ \Omega $ will always refer to an open set, most often of $ \mathbf R^n $, and $ I $ will always refer to an open interval.

\section{Basics}

We now start with some facts and theorems.

\begin{theorem}

    Let $ f : \Omega \subset \mathbf R^n \to \mathbf R^p $ and $ x : I \subset \mathbf R \to \Omega $ be differentiable functions.

    Then, for all $ t \in I $,
    \[
        (f \circ x)'(t) = \mathrm d f_{x(t)}(x'(t))
    \]

\end{theorem}
